About my strategy

My strategy trend-following. Here's a general outline of how this strategy could work Starting by analyzing the 1 Day chart to identify the overall trend. This can be done by looking at the highs and lows of the price action. If the price is consistently making higher highs and higher lows, then the trend is bullish. If the price is consistently making lower highs and lower lows, then the trend is bearish. Once the trend has been identified, look for opportunities to enter the trade in the direction of the trend. One way to do this is to wait for a pullback or retracement to the trendline or moving average. This can provide a good entry point with a favorable risk/reward ratio. To manage risk, set a stop-loss level below the recent swing low (for long positions) or above the recent swing high (for short positions). Take-profit levels can be set at key resistance or support levels or based on a risk/reward ratio between 1:1 to 1:2. Once the trade is open, monitor it closely and adjust the stop-loss and take-profit levels as needed. Trailing stops can also be used to lock in profits as the trade moves in your favor. When the price reaches your take-profit level or if the trend reverses, exit the trade and take your profits.
